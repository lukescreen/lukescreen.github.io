The GetStuffDone Method

The GetStuffDone method is composed of two techniques.
They are; Animedoro, and the Shitty Draft.

Animedoro

The Animedoro technique is a time stucture that balances Studies and Anime.

  • 40-60 mins of work.
  • 1 short episode of any show.

Timings

It's important to let the natural breaks/gaps in study occur.
This means breaks are taken at the end of a section or piece of work.
That's why the working range is 40-60 minutes; as long as your are inside that range, you can take your break.

Why is this better than Pomodoro?

  • The work period for Pomodoro is 25 minutes.
  • This doesn't give much time to complete a task when settling down is considered.
  • The 20 minute range of Animedoro also allows for work to flow naturally.
  • Pomodoro's rest period is only 5 minutes.
  • That's definitely not long enough to do anything rewarding/satisfying.

The Shitty Draft

The goal of the shitty draft is to brute force as much work as possible.

How does it prevent procrastination?

The easier the work feels, the less likely you are to procrastinate from doing it.

The Second Pass

This is where it gets clever...
When you are doing the work properly on the second pass, it is just editing existing work.
That helps you not get tired because it doesn't feel as much like doing work.

"I need to answer the last half." becomes "I need to edit my answers in the last half."

In Summary

This method won't make you hyper-productive, but it will hopefully help you enjoy working a bit more.
The only way to succeed is to work hard.
You could just watch anime, but you need to have the willpower to be proactive and thus productive.
